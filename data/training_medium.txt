# --- KNOWLEDGE (expanded) ---

Q: What is AI?
A: AI, or Artificial Intelligence, refers to computer systems designed to perform tasks that typically require human intelligence. This includes learning, reasoning, problem-solving, and understanding language.

Q: What is machine learning?
A: Machine learning is a subset of AI where computers learn from data instead of following explicit programming. The system finds patterns in examples and uses them to make predictions or decisions.

Q: What is a neural network?
A: A neural network is a computing system inspired by the human brain. It consists of interconnected nodes (artificial neurons) organized in layers that process information and learn patterns.

Q: What is deep learning?
A: Deep learning is a type of machine learning using neural networks with many layers (hence "deep"). It's particularly good at processing images, speech, and natural language.

Q: How do computers work?
A: Computers process information using electronic circuits that can be on or off (representing 1s and 0s). By combining billions of these switches and following instructions, they perform complex tasks.

Q: What is programming?
A: Programming is the process of writing instructions for computers. Programmers use languages like Python, JavaScript, or C++ to create software, apps, games, and AI systems.

Q: What is the internet?
A: The internet is a global network of connected computers. It allows people to share information, communicate, and access services from anywhere in the world.

Q: What is a computer virus?
A: A computer virus is malicious software that can copy itself and spread between computers, often causing damage or stealing information.

Q: What is encryption?
A: Encryption is the process of converting information into a coded format that can only be read by someone with the right key. It protects sensitive data.

Q: What is the cloud?
A: "The cloud" refers to servers and services accessed over the internet, rather than on your local computer. It allows storage and computing without physical hardware.

Q: What is an algorithm?
A: An algorithm is a step-by-step procedure for solving a problem or completing a task. It's like a recipe that computers follow.

Q: What is data?
A: Data is information in a form that computers can process - numbers, text, images, sounds, etc. It's the raw material that AI learns from.

Q: What is a database?
A: A database is an organized collection of data stored and accessed electronically. It allows efficient storage, retrieval, and management of information.

Q: What is software?
A: Software is the programs and applications that run on computers. Unlike hardware (physical components), software is intangible code that tells computers what to do.

Q: What is hardware?
A: Hardware refers to the physical components of a computer - the processor, memory, storage, screen, keyboard, etc. It's what you can touch.
